Cons To Gastric Sleeve

Gastric sleeve surgery is among the most successful and extensively embraced bariatric surgeries available, assisting thousands of individuals shed extra weight for many years. While safe and reliable weight loss surgical treatments exist, such as gastric bypass and stomach sleeve treatments have some disadvantages that must be carefully considered before making your decision on whether this treatment is right for you or not.

Gastric sleeve surgical treatment can not be undone as soon as finished; its effects can not be reversed when cut and stapled into an anorexic tube shape called a sleeve. For that reason, patients going through stomach sleeve needs to prepare to make significant lifestyle adjustments and follow all dietary recommendations for life after undergoing this kind of surgery.

Another concern associated with stomach sleeve surgery is reflux (in which acid from the stomach moves upward into the esophagus), even after weight-loss. Reflux can usually be treated utilizing medication, however some sleeve clients may require bypass surgery to manage symptoms successfully.

Some clients can develop disposing syndrome, in which food passes too quickly through the digestion tract and causes diarrhea or vomiting. Although less typical than many might fear, this condition generally solves itself with medication or diet changes.
